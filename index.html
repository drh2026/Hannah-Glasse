About This Archive

This digital portal contains 955 recipes extracted from Hannah Glasse's The Art of Cookery Made Plain and Easy (New Edition, 1803), one of the most influential English-language cookbooks ever published. First printed in 1747, it dominated British and American kitchens for over a century and remained in continuous print for nearly sixty years.

Glasse wrote explicitly for household servants and home cooks, rejecting the elaborate French terminology that dominated professional cookery. Her stated purpose was radical simplicity: "my design is to instruct the ignorant and unlearned, and in so plain and full a manner, that the most illiterate and ignorant person, who can but read, will know how to do every thing in Cookery well."

The 1803 edition represents the culmination of over fifty years of revisions and additions to the original 1747 text. It contains the earliest printed English recipes for curry, Yorkshire pudding, and piccalilli, alongside hundreds of recipes spanning roasting, sauces, made dishes, fish, soups, puddings, pies, preserves, wines, and household remedies.
